Between Two Lakes

They secretly began to meet at an abandoned shepherd's hut between two mountain lakes. Three months living in between, Estelle playing the obedient daughter by day and Matthieu the silent protector, but at night they were just two souls who found each other in a world set to keep them apart.

The Price of Love

But fate has a cruel sense of timing. Lord Beaumont had arranged for Estelle to be married to the son of a duke from Marseille. When she refused, her father locked her in the tower of his estate.

Matthieu, desperate and foolish with love, one stormy night, came to free her. The guards caught him climbing the wall of the tower. Infuriated, Lord Beaumont ordered Matthieu to be executed at dawn.

Estelle watched from her window, screaming until her voice was hoarse, while her father's heart remained stone. Within a week, she was pronounced dead of a broken heart, though servants whispered she'd willed herself to join Matthieu.

The Curse

A hundred years later, a farmer bought the land between the lakes. Two cows were born on the same night, one with markings like midnight sky, the other with a white star on her forehead. These cows were inseparable, always grazing side by side, always resting under the same tree.

When one died of old age, the other followed within a day. This pattern repeated for generations: two cows, always together, always dying within days of each other.

The villagers came to believe that Estelle and Matthieu were granted neither heaven nor hell but a state in between. Cursed to live as beasts, never to speak their love, never to touch as humans touch, but at least together.

Eternal Longing

To this day, the two lakes remain beneath the mountains off the coast of Nice. The locals call them the Lakes of Eternal Longing. Some say if you visit at sunset, you'll see two cows graze in the valley between them, always together, forever paying the price for the audacity to love across a line that society drew in the sand.

Some loves are neither blessed nor cursed but merely transmuted into something eternal, patient, and forever incomplete.
